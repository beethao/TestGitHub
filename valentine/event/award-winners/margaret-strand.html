Margaret Strand

Margaret Strand was only three and a half when her family received devastating news – their little girl had leukemia. Doctors ordered that she immediately start treatment and she underwent a program that included 798 days of chemotherapy, all before she turned six years old. During the physically taxing treatment Margaret’s strong character, upbeat attitude and relentless perseverance caught the attention of doctors and nurses. Not only has she never asked her parents “Why me” but she has been a source of encouragement and wisdom beyond her years. She is active with a number of North Texas organizations, including The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society, to raise awareness and money of childhood cancers.
